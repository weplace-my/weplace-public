# Weplace Government Grant Pitch

## Working Title
Weplace: A Neighborhood Social Commerce Platform for Inclusive Income and Lower Living Costs

## One-Line Positioning
Weplace helps neighbors buy, sell, support, and earn within the same local community, with a strong focus on low-barrier participation for home-based and disabled micro-entrepreneurs.

## Executive Summary
Weplace is a neighborhood-based social commerce platform built for Malaysian communities. It enables users to join a location-based digital community, discover nearby sellers, buy goods and homemade food, participate in local events, request help, and create small-scale income opportunities from home.

The platform is especially relevant for underserved groups such as disabled individuals, caregivers, retirees, and low-capital home-based entrepreneurs who may face barriers in traditional business setup. By reducing the need for rental, logistics, and setup costs, Weplace creates a more accessible path to income generation and community participation.

## Problem Statement
Many people have useful products, homemade food, skills, or services they can offer, but they face barriers such as:

- high startup costs
- limited visibility
- weak trust in online selling
- difficulty reaching nearby buyers
- lack of accessible earning options for disabled and home-based individuals

At the same time, many communities face higher living costs and limited access to practical, neighborhood-level support and affordable local deals.

## Solution
Weplace creates a hyperlocal platform where users can:

- join a verified location community
- discover local products, food, services, and vouchers
- buy from nearby sellers with lower friction
- create home-based microbusiness listings
- use community tools such as help requests and events
- support local disabled and home-based entrepreneurs through neighborhood demand

## Why It Matters
Weplace is not only a commerce app. It is a community-enablement platform.

Its value lies in:

- lowering entry barriers to micro-entrepreneurship
- creating inclusive earning opportunities from home
- reducing cost-of-living pressure through local exchange
- helping communities become more self-supporting
- enabling trust through neighborhood identity and proximity

## Social Impact Angle
Weplace supports inclusive economic participation for:

- disabled individuals who may prefer or require home-based work
- caregivers who need flexible earning opportunities
- retirees who want low-pressure side income
- homemakers and small food entrepreneurs
- low-capital individuals testing business ideas

This makes Weplace relevant not only for digital commerce development, but also for:

- community empowerment
- social inclusion
- accessibility-led economic participation
- local resilience and shared prosperity

## Accessibility and Inclusion Narrative
Weplace can be positioned as an accessible digital enablement tool that helps disabled users participate in the economy with dignity.

Instead of relying on conventional employment structures, users can:

- start from home
- sell to nearby communities
- reduce delivery and operational burden
- build confidence through smaller and more manageable demand

The platform's local-first design can also improve trust, which is especially important for vulnerable or underserved communities.

## Key Product Features
- location-based onboarding and QR join flow
- local market discovery
- seller dashboard and order management
- homemade food and small-seller listing support
- priority support concept for special cases
- community events and help-request features
- local trust through shared neighborhood context

## Differentiation
Compared with general marketplaces, Weplace is different because it is:

- neighborhood-first instead of nationwide-first
- community-driven instead of purely transactional
- home-based seller friendly
- lower-friction for micro-entrepreneurs
- potentially more inclusive for disabled and underserved groups

## Target Beneficiaries
- disabled community members seeking home-based income
- B40 and lower-middle-income households
- homemakers and retirees
- home cooks and small food sellers
- neighborhood buyers looking for better local value
- community residents seeking practical local support

## Proposed Outcomes
Short-term outcomes:

- more local sellers onboarded
- more neighborhood transactions completed
- more disabled and home-based users participating as sellers
- higher discovery of local products and services

Medium-term outcomes:

- stronger community-level economic circulation
- lower barriers to small business testing
- better household resilience through extra income channels

Long-term outcomes:

- stronger neighborhood micro-economies
- better digital inclusion for underserved groups
- meaningful contribution toward lower living-cost pressure through local exchange

## Suggested Metrics for Grant Reporting
- number of registered locations
- number of active sellers
- number of disabled or home-based sellers onboarded
- number of local transactions completed
- repeat buyer rate
- average distance between seller and buyer
- estimated logistics savings from local transactions
- number of community help or event interactions

## Funding Use Proposal
Grant funding can be used for:

- product refinement and accessibility improvements
- onboarding programs for disabled and home-based sellers
- community pilot deployment
- awareness and adoption campaigns
- partnership programs with local associations or agencies
- trust, moderation, and support tooling

## Pilot Proposal
Weplace can start with a focused pilot in selected communities, for example:

- apartments or gated communities
- urban residential clusters
- communities with active local committees
- partner groups supporting disabled individuals or caregivers

Pilot goals:

- validate demand
- measure transaction behavior
- refine onboarding and seller education
- document social and economic impact

Government support can help accelerate:

- inclusive digital entrepreneurship
- accessibility-focused income generation
- neighborhood economic resilience
- cost-of-living support through local commerce

This aligns well with initiatives around:

- digital economy participation
- community wellbeing

- micro-entrepreneurship

Weplace is a practical platform with a human-centered mission: to help people start small, earn locally, and support each other within the same community.

With the right support, it can become a meaningful tool for inclusive growth, especially for disabled and home-based individuals who need lower-barrier ways to participate in the economy.
